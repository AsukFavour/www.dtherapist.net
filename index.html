How We Do It

At DTherapist, we aim to foster a compassionate, safe environment where you can thrive. Whether you seek professional counseling or simply need a place to express yourself anonymously, we are here to support you every step of the way.

Group Therapy

Involves a group of people who share a common problem. It is often used to treat anxiety, depression, and other mental health disorders. Itprovides social support, education, and skills training in a group setting.

Psychodynamic Therapy

Explores the unconscious mind and how it affects behavior. It is often used to treat personality disorders and other mental health disorders

Family, couples/marriage Therapy (FCMT)

helps improve communication, resolve conflicts, and strengthen relationships within families or between partners.

Interpersonal therapy (IPT)

IPT is a type of therapy that focuses on helping people resolve interpersonal problems. It is often used to treat depression and anxiety.

Cognitive-Behavioural Therapy (CBT)

CBT is a type of therapy that focuses on changing the way people think and behave. It is often used to treat anxiety, depression, and other mental health disorders.

Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT)

DBT focuses on helping people regulate their emotions and improve their interpersonal skills. It is often used to treat borderline personality disorder. It combines CBT with mindfulness techniques to manage emotions and behaviours.

Specialized Therapies

  • Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing (EMDR): Treats trauma and PTSD.
  • Trauma-Focused Cognitive-Behavioral (TF-CBT): Addresses trauma and PTSD in children and teens.
  • Mindfulness-Based Therapies: Uses mindfulness to reduce stress and improve emotional regulation.
  • Art Therapy(AT): Uses creative expression to explore and express emotions.
  • Play Therapy (PT): Uses play to explore and address emotional and behavioral issues in children.

Welcome to DTherapist & DAnonymous


In today's fast-paced world, mental health issues, loss, and grief can affect anyone, regardless of age or background. The struggle to cope with these challenges can be overwhelming, leaving individuals feeling isolated, uncertain, and in need of support. That's why we created DTherapist, a comprehensive online platform dedicated to providing accessible, compassionate, and specialized mental health services for adults, children, and adolescents.

We offer a variety of therapy options, including individual therapy, group therapy, and couples’ therapy. We also offer a variety of support groups, including groups for people who are struggling with anxiety, depression, grief, and loss. Our support groups provide a safe and supportive space where people can connect with others who understand what they are going through.

Frequently Asked Questions

These FAQs help address common concerns and questions for new and prospective users of your platform.

What types of counseling do you offer?

We provide a range of therapy and counseling services, including support for anxiety, depression, relationship challenges, stress management, grief, career issues, and personal growth. Our licensed therapists are available for one-on-one virtual sessions tailored to your needs.

Is the anonymous experience-sharing platform really confidential?

Yes, absolutely. Our anonymous space allows you to share your thoughts, experiences, and stories without revealing your identity. We prioritize your privacy and ensure that all conversations remain confidential and respectful.

How do I book a therapy session?

Booking a session with one of our licensed therapists is simple. You can browse available counselors based on your needs and schedule a time that works for you through our platform. Sessions are conducted online for your convenience.

Can I contribute to the anonymous experience-sharing platform?

Yes! As a contributor, you can offer positive and constructive feedback to those who share their stories anonymously. We encourage thoughtful, supportive contributions to help others feel understood and encouraged in their journey.
